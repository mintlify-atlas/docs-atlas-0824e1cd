## Welcome to DIDG

The DIDG System is your central hub for accessing course materials, tracking grades, and managing your academic resources. This guide will help you navigate the platform effectively.

## Getting Started

### Logging In

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Login Page">
    Go to the DIDG platform login page. Students use simplified authentication without 2FA.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Your Credentials">
    Enter your registered email address and password.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Access Your Dashboard">
    Upon successful login, you'll be redirected to `/dashboard` or the public course catalog.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Students do not require two-factor authentication (2FA). The system automatically detects your role and provides direct access.
</Note>

## Browsing Courses

### Viewing Available Courses

The courses page (`/courses`) displays all active semesters and their associated subjects.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Courses">
    Click on the "Cursos" or "Centro de Recursos" link from the navigation menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Browse by Semester">
    Courses are organized by semester (e.g., "2024-2"). Each semester shows all active subjects.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a Subject">
    Click on any subject card to view its ayudantía sessions and materials.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Understanding the Course Card

Each course card displays:
- **Subject Name**: The full name of the course
- **Subject Code**: A unique identifier (e.g., "INF-123")
- **Icon**: A book icon indicating educational content
- **Description**: Brief overview of available materials

<Tip>
  Use the breadcrumb navigation at the top of each page to quickly return to the course catalog.
</Tip>

## Accessing Ayudantía Materials

### Viewing Ayudantía Sessions

When you select a subject, you'll see a timeline of all ayudantía (tutorial) sessions.

**Timeline Features:**
- Sessions are sorted by date (most recent first)
- Each session card shows:
  - Title (e.g., "Ayudantía 1 - SQL")
  - Date of the session
  - Description with topics covered (supports Markdown)
  - Available resources (PDFs, videos)

### Downloading Materials

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the Session">
    Find the ayudantía session you need on the subject page at `/courses/[subjectId]`.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Available Resources">
    Each session may include:
    - PDF files (lecture notes, guides)
    - ZIP files (code examples, projects)
    - Video URLs (YouTube recordings)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Download or View">
    Click the download button for PDFs/ZIPs, or click the video link to watch recordings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  All downloads are tracked for analytics. This helps instructors understand which materials are most valuable.
</Note>

### Filtering Ayudantías

The `AyudantiaList` component provides filtering options:
- **Search**: Find sessions by title or description keywords
- **Filter by Type**: Show only PDFs or only videos
- **Sort**: Sessions are automatically sorted by date

## Bookmarking Resources

### Saving Ayudantías for Later

Bookmark important sessions to quickly access them from your profile.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to an Ayudantía">
    Open any subject page and locate the session you want to save.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click the Bookmark Icon">
    Look for the bookmark/save icon on the ayudantía card (only visible when logged in).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Access Saved Items">
    Go to your profile page (`/profile`) to view all bookmarked ayudantías under the "Saved Ayudantías" tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Bookmarks are stored in the `ayudantia_bookmarks` table with your user ID, so they persist across sessions.
</Tip>

### Bookmarking Extra Resources

Beyond ayudantías, you can also bookmark items from the resource library:

1. Navigate to `/resources`
2. Browse or search for tools, tutorials, or code snippets
3. Click the bookmark icon on any resource card
4. View saved resources in your profile under "Bookmarked Resources"

## Viewing Your Grades

### Accessing the Grades Page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Grades">
    Click on "Mis Calificaciones" in the navigation menu or go to `/grades`.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Your Enrollments">
    The page displays all subjects you're enrolled in. For each subject, you'll see:
    - Subject name and code
    - All recorded grades for evaluations (e.g., "Certamen 1")
    - Current average score
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Understand Your Performance">
    Grades are color-coded:
    - **Green**: Score ≥ 4.0 (passing)
    - **Red**: Score < 4.0 (needs improvement)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Grade Calculation

- The system fetches grades from the `grades` table linked to your enrollments
- **Average**: Sum of all scores divided by the number of evaluations
- **Weight**: Some systems may use weighted averages (check the `weight` field)

<Note>
  If you see "No hay notas registradas aún," your instructor hasn't uploaded grades for that subject yet.
</Note>

## Using the Grade Simulator

<Accordion title="What is the Grade Simulator?">
  The grade simulator (if implemented as a client component) allows you to:
  - Input hypothetical scores for upcoming evaluations
  - Calculate projected final grades
  - Plan how well you need to perform on future exams
  
  This feature helps you set realistic academic goals and track progress.
</Accordion>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access the Simulator">
    Look for a "Simulador de Notas" button or tab on the grades page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Evaluation Data">
    Input the name, weight, and score for each evaluation (both completed and planned).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Calculate Results">
    The simulator will show your projected final grade based on the weighted average.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use the simulator before major exams to understand the minimum score you need to achieve your target grade.
</Tip>

## Managing Your Profile

### Viewing Profile Information

Your profile page (`/profile`) uses a tabbed interface powered by the `ProfileTabs` component.

**Available Tabs:**

1. **Profile Info**
   - Full name
   - RUT (national ID)
   - Email address
   - Role (student)

2. **Bookmarked Resources**
   - All saved items from the resource library
   - Quick access links to files or URLs

3. **Saved Ayudantías**
   - All bookmarked tutorial sessions
   - Direct links to subject pages

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Profile">
    Click your name or profile icon in the navigation menu, or go to `/profile`.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Switch Between Tabs">
    Use the tab navigation to view different sections of your profile.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Manage Bookmarks">
    Remove bookmarks by clicking the unbookmark icon on any saved item.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Navigating the Platform

### Main Navigation Paths

- **Home** (`/`): Landing page with an overview of the platform
- **Courses** (`/courses`): Browse all active subjects and ayudantías
- **Resources** (`/resources`): Access the shared resource library
- **Grades** (`/grades`): View your academic performance
- **Profile** (`/profile`): Manage bookmarks and view personal info
- **Projects** (`/projects`): Explore instructor projects and portfolios

### Breadcrumb Navigation

The `Breadcrumbs` component appears at the top of most pages:
- Shows your current location in the site hierarchy
- Provides quick links back to parent pages
- Example: `Cursos > Base de Datos > Ayudantía 3`

<Tip>
  Use breadcrumbs to quickly navigate back without using the browser's back button.
</Tip>

## Common UI Elements

### Cards and Hover Effects

Most interactive elements use card-based layouts with hover effects:
- **Subject Cards**: Lift slightly on hover with a secondary color glow
- **Ayudantía Cards**: Show action buttons when you hover over them
- **Resource Cards**: Display edit/delete options for admins, bookmark for students

### Icons and Visual Indicators

- **BookOpen**: Indicates courses and subjects
- **Calendar**: Shows semester information
- **Video**: Indicates video content is available
- **FileText**: Indicates downloadable PDF/ZIP files
- **Bookmark**: Save/unsave functionality

### Theme Support

The platform supports light and dark modes:
- Background colors adapt via `bg-surface` and `bg-background`
- Text colors use `text-text-main` and `text-text-muted`
- Borders use `border-text-main/10` for theme-aware visibility

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="I can't see any courses">
  **Possible Causes:**
  - No semesters are marked as active (`is_active = true`)
  - You're viewing a semester with no subjects assigned
  - Network or database connection issue
  
  **Solution:**
  Contact your instructor or system administrator to verify course availability.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="My bookmarks aren't saving">
  **Possible Causes:**
  - You're not logged in (bookmarks require authentication)
  - Database permissions issue
  
  **Solution:**
  Ensure you're logged in. Try logging out and back in. If the issue persists, contact support.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I don't see my grades">
  **Possible Causes:**
  - You're not enrolled in any subjects
  - Grades haven't been uploaded yet
  - Enrollment data is missing
  
  **Solution:**
  Check with your instructor to confirm enrollment and grade publication status.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Videos won't play">
  **Possible Causes:**
  - Invalid video URL
  - YouTube link is private or restricted
  - Browser blocking embedded content
  
  **Solution:**
  Try opening the video URL in a new tab. If it's private, contact the instructor.
</Accordion>

## Getting Help

If you encounter issues or have questions:

1. Check this documentation first
2. Contact your instructor via the contact form (`/contact`)
3. Report technical issues to the system administrator

<Note>
  The contact form at `/contact` sends messages directly to the instructor, who can view them in the admin dashboard at `/dashboard/messages`.
</Note>
